LA COURT
Online Services

The Los Angeles Superior Court provides many online resources. You can check on your jury duty, pay your ticket, check court records and more.

  • Online Services by Court Division

  • Case Access - Appellate

    You can get a summary of your appellate case. All you need is your case number.

    Tentative Rulings - Appellate

    You can search by case number or location for tentative rulings made in appellate cases.

    Case Access - Civil

    You can get a summary of your civil, small claims, family law and probate case. All you need is your case number.

    Case Document Images - Civil, Small Claims, Family Law, and Probate

    You can download scanned images of case documents.

    Court Reservation System (CRS) - Civil

    You can make a reservation for a civil unlimited case online (for selected courthouses).

    Case Calendar - Civil

    You can access up to 14 days of a courtroom's calendar to find your court date for general jurisdiction civil, limited jurisdiction civil, family law and probate calendars. In compliance with Code of Civil Procedure section 1161.2, Unlawful Detainer case information is not available to the public for the first 60 days.

    Courtroom Information - Civil

    You can view published courtroom-specific information for civil courts.

    Filing Court Locator - Civil

    You can find the courthouse location where you may file your civil, family law, probate or small claims case using a zip code or city/community.

    Interpreter Request - Civil

    You can request an interpreter for your civil case.

    Search for Case Number by Name - Civil

    You can search parties of record and participants for civil, small claims, family law and probate cases to find your case number.

    Tentative Rulings - Civil

    The court is currently making a transition to use this new Tentative Rulings website. Judicial officers who have made the transition will have their tentative rulings published here.

    Government Agency Criminal Record Online Request - Criminal

    This is to be used by government agencies only for official requests of court criminal records. Requests submitted through this portal by individuals or non-government agencies will not be fulfilled.

    Calendar Search - Criminal

    You can search for defendants in criminal cases by name.

    Case Summary - Criminal

    You can get a summary of your case. All you need is your case number.

    Search for Case Number by Defendant Name - Criminal

    You can search for defendants in criminal cases by name.

    Case Access - Family Law

    You can get a summary of your civil, small claims, family law and probate case. All you need is your case number.

    Case Calendar - Family Law

    You can access up to 14 days of a courtroom's calendar to find your court date for general jurisdiction civil, limited jurisdiction civil, family law and probate calendars. In compliance with Code of Civil Procedure section 1161.2, Unlawful Detainer case information is not available to the public for the first 60 days.

    Our Children First Program - Family Law

    You can complete the Court's orientation to mediation on-line (family law cases). All you need is your case number to access the program.

    Divorce Judgment Documents - Family Law

    You can order copies of divorce judgments online. Copies of imaged divorce judgments from the County Records Center/Archives may now be ordered via this web site. A simple question and answer program will assist you in submitting your order. You will need a valid credit card and information about the divorce case.

    Filing Court Locator - Family Law

    You can find the courthouse location where you may file your civil, family law, probate or small claims case using a zip code or city/community.

    Interpreter Request - Family Law

    You can request an interpreter for your Family Law case.

    Search for Case Number by Name - Family Law

    You can search parties of record and participants for civil, small claims, family law and probate cases to find your case number.

    Tentative Rulings - Family Law

    You can search by case number or location for tentative rulings made in family law cases.

    Probate Efiling

    The Probate Division has implemented mandatory electronic filing (efiling) for attorneys and provided the option of efiling to self-represented litigants. Probate matters are heard at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse and the Michael D. Antonovich Antelope Valley Courthouse.

    Case Access - Probate

    You can get a summary of your civil, small claims, family law and probate case. All you need is your case number.

    Case Calendar - Probate

    You can access up to 14 days of a courtroom's calendar to find your court date for general jurisdiction civil, limited jurisdiction civil, family law and probate calendars.
